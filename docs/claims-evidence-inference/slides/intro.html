Claims, Evidence, and Inferences

Goals:
  • Identify claims, evidence, and inferences.
  • Notice when a claim is unsupported, weakly supported, or overstated relative to the evidence.
  • Make reasonable judgments about whether inferences are sound or not, relative to the evidence.
  • Differentiate the act of fact checking per se from recognizing claims which could be checked. You might not always have the time or ability to check everything (none of us can prove "everything"), but you should be aware of whether the claims that you or others are making can, in principle, be supported by evidence, and whether or not such evidence has been provided.
