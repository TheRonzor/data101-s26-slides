Example 2 - Medical

  • Claim: Vaccinations are linked to autism.
  • Evidence: A study published by (former doctor) Andrew Wakefield in 1998.
  • Inference: We should not vaccinate our children.
  • Many believed (and some still do) that the inference was correct. The paper, at first glance, might seem "legit". It was published by a doctor after all! In this sense, the inference is reasonable.
  • The paper was later found to be fraudulent and riddled with methodological flaws. It was officially retracted by the publisher about 10 years later, several of the other authors withdrew their support, and Wakefield had his medical license revoked.
