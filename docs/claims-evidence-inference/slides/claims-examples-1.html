Example 1 - Math

  • Claim: There are 4 prime numbers between 1 and 10.
  • Evidence: One can easily check that there are exactly 4 prime numbers between 1 and 10, namely, 2, 3, 5, and 7.
  • Inference: There are exactly 4 prime numbers in any 10-number interval.
  • This is not a reasonable inference and it is also flat-out wrong. In mathematics, we do not prove things by example unless we can list all possible examples. Moreover, one can readily find an extreme counterexample - there are no prime numbers in the 10-number interval between 114 and 123.
