Example 3 - Physics

  • Claim: It is possible that regions of spacetime exist which are so densely packed with matter that it would all collapse into an infinitely dense point and the resulting gravitational force would prevent anything, even light, from ever escaping.
  • Evidence: Originally, just a mathematical theory (Einstein's theory of General Relativity).
  • Inference: Black holes exist.
  • Einstein himself did not believe this. He thought it was a mathematical artifact that couldn't possibly reflect physical reality.
  • Since then, much indirect (gravitational effects on other visible bodies) and direct (imaging of objects such as M87* and Sagittarius A*) evidence has been accumulated.
